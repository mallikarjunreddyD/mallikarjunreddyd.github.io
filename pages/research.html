My research lies at the intersection of blockchain systems, privacy-preserving protocols, tokenized digital assets, and trustworthy distributed computing. A common thread across these projects is the design of practical systems that combine security, transparency, and deployability in real-world environments.

Zero-Knowledge Proofs for Autonomous Truck Platooning

We are designing an end-to-end protocol for autonomous truck platooning using zero-knowledge techniques, with a strong emphasis on privacy-preserving identity verification, authenticated access, and operational trust in mixed-fleet environments.

Building on prior work in blockchain-empowered authentication for platooning, this direction studies how trucks can prove eligibility and compliance without exposing sensitive business or vehicle information, while still enabling efficient joining, coordination, and record integrity.

Tokenization of ESG Assets

This work explores how environmental, social, and governance-linked assets can be represented on blockchain infrastructures in a way that improves transparency, auditability, programmability, and market access.

The broader goal is to design tokenization frameworks that align digital asset models with compliance requirements, credible reporting, and trusted participation across stakeholders.

Portfolio Tokenization

I am also working on portfolio tokenization, focusing on how baskets of assets can be structured, represented, and managed through blockchain-based systems.

This direction examines how tokenized portfolios can support fractional ownership, transparent governance, and efficient lifecycle management while remaining compatible with regulatory and institutional expectations.

Broadcast Communication

This research investigates secure and efficient broadcast communication, especially in settings where devices must authenticate messages continuously and reliably under constrained resources.

The focus is on strengthening trust, resilience, and scalability in distributed communication settings where both performance and security are critical.

Federated Learning

My work in federated learning studies decentralized training frameworks where privacy, trust, and accountability must be preserved across participating entities.

A central theme here is combining collaborative learning with secure aggregation, transparent coordination, and verifiable system behavior, particularly in environments where data sharing is restricted.

Research Vision

Across these areas, my objective is to build trustworthy decentralized infrastructures that make complex digital interactions more private, auditable, and practically deployable.
